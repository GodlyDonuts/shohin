Psi · 100M Build Plan · 2026-07

Build the best 100M model of 2026 on PyTorch — not the custom stack — and win on commonsense, not knowledge.

The single strategic call: the entire 2026 edge over SmolLM2 comes from things the Psi C++ stack cannot do quickly — pretraining-time logit distillation, bf16 tensor-core throughput, and 8-GPU data parallelism. Spend the compute budget on the model, not on rebuilding infrastructure. Keep ternary/C++ as a separate craft track and port the winner to it afterward.

01 Headline & reality check

Yes, a 2026 100M model beats SmolLM2 — but only on the axes capacity allows.

"Crush" is true for commonsense, language modeling, and instruction-following. It is false for world knowledge and math, which are pinned by parameter count no matter the recipe. Grade the model only on what it can actually win.

Winnable — better data + distillation move these
  • HellaSwag, PIQA, ARC-e/c
  • WinoGrande, OpenBookQA, CommonsenseQA
  • Instruction-following (IFEval)
  • Per-token data efficiency
Capacity-capped — report, don't chase
  • MMLU (factual recall)
  • TriviaQA (closed-book knowledge)
  • GSM8K / MATH (multi-step math)
  • Long-chain reasoning

The physics is the reason: LMs store roughly 2 bits of knowledge per parameter (Allen-Zhu, arXiv 2404.05405), so ~130M params holds ~30 MB of facts — a sliver of Wikipedia. That is why SmolLM2-135M scores TriviaQA 4.1 and MMLU near the cloze floor, and no recipe fixes it. Distillation transfers a teacher's skills and distribution, not its stored facts into a body that physically can't hold them.

Tokenizer note that pays off: matching the teacher's vocab is not cosmetic — it lets you use full soft-label KD without a vocabulary-projection hack. Pick the teacher's tokenizer first, then build the student around it.

Pretrain — stable

Distillation — the decisive lever

Method: offline soft-label (logit) KD — precompute a converged teacher's top-k truncated logits (top-0.95, k≈50), train with KL loss at α≈0.9 KD weight under the WSD schedule (arXiv 2410.16215; LFM2's tempered Top-K objective). MSE loss hurts (−7.6%); online KD is worse than offline converged-teacher logits.

Teacher: a strong but close model — Qwen3-1.7B or Llama-3.2-1B, not a 30B frontier model. A 130M student is ~7–13% of a 1–2B teacher, right at the empirical effectiveness floor (student should be ≥~10% of teacher). A larger teacher widens the capacity gap and helps less.

Two honest asterisks on the recipe

KD is unproven below 330M. The design-space study only tested down to 330M and found larger students benefit more. At 130M the marginal gain of logit-KD over simply training on the teacher's generated text may be small — so DATA quality is the co-equal lever, not an afterthought.

Reasoning distillation is the wrong bet here. "Scale or Reason?" (arXiv 2509.22193) tests down to 0.5B: reasoning captures the Pareto frontier only ≥7B, and — critically — multiple-choice tasks (our whole commonsense suite) do not benefit from reasoning regardless of domain. Format finding, not just capacity.

03 The custom-stack decision — the crux

Use PyTorch for this model. Keep the C++ ternary stack as a parallel craft track.

The user said "forget what we've done." Taken seriously, the engineering math is unambiguous: the winning recipe is a distillation-infra + high-throughput-data problem, and the custom stack is fp32, single-GPU, copy-bound, with no KD infra — the axes we most need to win on are exactly the ones it is least equipped to deliver quickly.

Option A

Build it all on the custom C++ stack

  • Must build: bf16/tensor-core GEMM
  • Multi-GPU data-parallel (8×H100)
  • Offline teacher-logit KD pipeline + top-k storage + KL loss
  • Throughput-grade dataloader / sharding
  • Long-context + RoPE + GQA kernels
~8–14 person-weeks of infra before a competitive model trains; fp32-no-tensor-cores means ~5–15× less H100 throughput → 5–15× fewer tokens at equal wall-clock, forfeiting the overtraining lever. High risk, wrong budget.
Recommended
Option B

PyTorch for the model, C++ as a craft track

  • Use: TorchTitan / nanoGPT-class trainer, FSDP2
  • Flash-attention, bf16, mature multi-GPU DP
  • Existing KD + teacher-logit tooling
  • Iterate on DATA and distillation — where the science risk actually is
  • Ship a SmolLM2-beating model in days, not months
The mission isn't abandoned — the Psi stack's real novelty is capability-per-bit via ternary at inference, which is orthogonal to this benchmark race and can consume the trained weights later.
The hybrid — and the honest boundary of this claim

Train and win on PyTorch → then ternary-QAT the winner on the custom C++/CUDA kernel as the differentiated deliverable (a world-class-per-bit 130M model no framework ships). This keeps the from-scratch stack investment aimed at its actual edge. Note the engineering case for B stands on its own; it is not smuggled in from the benchmark evidence — the benchmarks only say which axes are winnable, the build-vs-buy is a separate throughput argument that happens to point the same way.

04 Compute, time & cost

The training run is not the bottleneck. Teacher-logit precompute and iteration are.

Core math: 6·N·D with N=1.3e8, D=2e11 ≈ 1.6e20 FLOPs, inside the 2–3e20 budget. A 130M model on 8×H100 in bf16 runs at hundreds-of-thousands of aggregate tok/s — the ~200B-token pretrain is roughly a single overnight run. The real cost sinks are one-time teacher-logit generation and the science iterations around data mix.

The single overnight main run is the smallest cost; budget discipline is about limiting ablation sprawl, not the flagship train.

05 The first concrete milestone

Match SmolLM2 with a no-distillation baseline before spending on KD.

De-risk the boring parts (data pipeline, arch, eval harness) with a clean from-scratch run first. Only add distillation once the baseline reproduces the bar on our own harness — so any later gain is attributable.

1

Pin the eval harness

Stand up the fixed commonsense suite (HellaSwag, ARC-e/c, PIQA, WinoGrande, OBQA, CSQA) and re-run SmolLM2-135M on it ourselves — never compare to quoted aggregates. This is the scoreboard for everything after.

2

Baseline pretrain — no KD

~130M arch, ~100–150B tokens of the Nemotron-CC-HQ / DCLM / FineWeb-Edu mix, WSD schedule. Goal: land within a couple points of SmolLM2-135M on the suite. This proves data + arch + infra before any teacher is involved.

3

Add KD on top — attribute the lift

Re-run identical config with offline logit KD from the chosen ~1–2B teacher. If the commonsense mean moves meaningfully over the baseline, the distillation thesis is validated at our scale — the one thing the literature does not yet prove below 330M. If it doesn't, we've spent little and learned the real answer.

Why this ordering

It isolates the single unproven variable (KD below 330M) as a cheap A/B, and guarantees we never attribute a data-mix win to distillation or vice-versa. If the baseline can't reach SmolLM2, no amount of KD will — better to find that out on a half-budget run.

06 Risks & the biggest uncertainty

Mostly manageable — one variable dominates.

Medium

Data-quality edge shrinks at 130M

open-sci-ref: dataset-quality gaps are smaller at 130M than at scale. Our 200B-vs-2T token deficit may be harder to erase with data quality alone — raising the burden on KD.

Medium

Teacher / student vocab mismatch

Full soft-label KD needs a shared tokenizer. Locking the student to the teacher's BPE early removes this; getting it wrong forces a lossy logit-projection hack.

Low

Throughput / infra on PyTorch

Well-trodden. FSDP2 + flash-attn + bf16 on 8×H100 is standard; the 200B-token run is a known quantity, not a research risk.

High

Chasing the wrong axes

The most expensive failure mode is spending budget on MMLU/GSM8K/long-CoT. Discipline: keep them off the scoreboard, report only for no-regression.

The single biggest uncertainty

Does pretraining logit-KD actually help a 130M student?

Every "clean beat SmolLM2" data point in the literature is either a larger model (Qwen3-0.6B, LFM2.5-350M, Gemma-3-270M) or an unproven extrapolation — KD was tested only down to 330M, and the design-space finding is that smaller students benefit less. Our headline win depends on KD paying off at ~2.5× below the smallest tested scale. Milestone 3 exists to answer this on a cheap A/B before the full spend. If the answer is no, the honest fallback is a data-quality-and-post-train win of a few points — real, but not a "crush."
