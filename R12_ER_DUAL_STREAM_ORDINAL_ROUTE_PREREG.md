## 1. Frozen diagnosis

Marginal-route v1.1 repairs the hard-route gradient starvation. On 8,000
family-disjoint old-training probe rows it reaches 90.938% exact relation
packets/joints, 97.062% states, and 98.537% answers. Oracle-route transport and
neutral-namespace alpha invariance are each 8,000/8,000. It nevertheless fails
the immutable complete-witness gate at 7,194/8,000 = 89.925%, six rows below
threshold, and is rejected before fresh data.

An independent immutable-evidence audit localizes all 806 row failures to one
after-witness pointer. Before-witness pointers are 107,764/107,764 exact. The
active occurrence-level rate is 214,722/215,528 = 99.626%; 202 residuals choose
another occurrence of the correct symbol and 604 choose an adjacent symbol.
Cardinality four is exact. Most residuals occur in the fourth active rule and
underrepresented cardinality/rule-count combinations. This is a local ordered-
slot error, not failed identity transport, insufficient capacity, or recurrent
execution.

## 2. Single admitted change

V1.2 replaces independent witness-slot routing with a differentiable ordinal
route lattice. For each possible cardinality `N`:

1. the structural stream identifies opaque candidates without reading their
   bytes;
2. the model-owned record-role distribution selects semantic rule records;
3. the lattice enumerates strictly order-preserving paths for `2N` witness
   slots through local candidates;
4. a rule record has `2N+1` candidates, so learned path probability chooses
   exactly one excluded candidate, expected to be the opcode;
5. a declaration record has `2N` candidates and remains a matched role-routing
   distractor;
6. predicted cardinality probabilities marginalize the four lattices rather
   than supplying cardinality from the host; and
7. the unchanged exact whole-symbol equality bus converts the learned before
   and after route marginals into relation logits.

No target range, symbol dictionary, delimiter parse, source outcome, executor
result, state, answer, retry, or repair enters inference. Candidate order and
monotonicity are architectural. Record assignment, cardinality, opcode
exclusion, event binding, HALT, query, and the final packet remain model-owned.
